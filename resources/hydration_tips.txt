Proper hydration is essential for physical performance, cognitive function, digestion, and temperature regulation. Users frequently ask how much water to drink, how to detect dehydration, and ways to stay hydrated throughout the day.

RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Adult women: 2.0–2.7 liters per day.
- Adult men: 2.5–3.7 liters per day.
- Increase fluid intake during heat, exercise, or illness.
- Include water-rich foods such as fruits and vegetables.
- Drink small amounts regularly rather than large volumes at once.
- Consider electrolyte drinks during long-duration exercise or heavy sweating.

COMMON PROBLEMS:
- Forgetting to drink water consistently.
- Feeling thirsty late in the day.
- Dark-colored urine indicating dehydration.
- Fatigue or headaches from low fluid intake.
